Our Heritage & Legacy

"A century of spiritual awakening and selfless service."

History of Ramakrishna Math, Bengaluru

1901: The Beginning

The movement in Bengaluru began in March 1901 with the formation of the Vedanta Society. A group of ardent devotees, inspired by the message of Sri Ramakrishna and the call of Swami Vivekananda, sought to establish a permanent spiritual anchor in the city.

1904: Founding the Centre

Heeding the request of the devotees, Srimat Swami Ramakrishnanandaji—who was then at Madras—established the permanent centre of the Ramakrishna Order in Bengaluru. The Math officially came into existence in 1904, initially operating from a rented building.

1906: The Foundation Stone

On 20 August 1906, Swami Abhedananda laid the foundation stone for the current Ashrama building. In a sacred ceremony, a Sanskrit copy of the Bhagavad Gita, pictures of the Holy Trio, and the Pancha Loha (five sacred metals) were consecrated beneath the cornerstone.

1909: Inauguration at Basavanagudi

Construction was completed by the end of 1908. On 20 January 1909, Srimat Swami Brahmananda, the first President of the Ramakrishna Order, formally inaugurated the Ashrama building in the presence of Swami Ramakrishnananda and Sister Devamata.

Our Inspiration

Sri Ramakrishna

The Prophet of the Modern Age

Through intense spiritual practices, Sri Ramakrishna relived the entire range of spiritual experiences of various faiths. He expressed the quintessence of his realizations in a simple, profound dictum: "Yato mat, tato path" (As many faiths, so many paths), demonstrating that all religions are valid pathways to the same Divine Reality.

Sri Sarada Devi

The Holy Mother

Endearingly known as 'Holy Mother', Sri Sarada Devi is the spiritual consort of Sri Ramakrishna. She embodied universal motherhood and boundless compassion, serving as the guiding force for the Ramakrishna Order after the Master's passing, accepting and blessing all without distinction.

Swami Vivekananda

The Awakener of Souls

The foremost disciple of Sri Ramakrishna, Swami Vivekananda carried the message of Vedanta to the world. He synthesized the ancient paths of Yoga and formulated the twin ideals of the Order: "Atmano mokshartham jagaddhitaya cha"—striving for one's own salvation and dedicating life to the welfare of the world.

The Twin Ideals

"Atmano Mokshartham Jagaddhitaya Cha"

The Ramakrishna Math operates on the principle of Shiva Jnane Jiva Seva—serving humanity as a form of worship. Whether it is spiritual discourse or humanitarian relief, every act is conducted as a spiritual discipline to realize the Divine within.
